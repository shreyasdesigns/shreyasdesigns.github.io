The Complete Review Workflow

Document Viewer

Enterprise Construction Large-scale workflows Documents Oracle Aconex

The Vision

Architecting a unified Document Viewer to bridge the gap between 2D drawings, 3D models, and Reality Capture. This project transformed a fragmented ecosystem into a single, context-aware workspace—minimizing friction for large-scale construction workflows.

Problem - The Fregmentation Tax

Enterprise users were paying a 'cognitive tax' by juggling disconnected tools. Reviewing a single document meant navigating a maze of viewers, workflow modules, and external coordination layers. A spiderweb of non-cohesive modules to switch, remember and coordinate between for a simple task - reviewing documents.

  • Review workflows were split across viewer, issues, and workflow modules
  • No clear distinction between viewing vs acting, increasing error rates
  • Collaboration lacked context
  • Comparing documents required manual navigation

The result was a high risk of error and a 'visibility vacuum' where collaboration lacked spatial context.

System Thinking - The Document-Centric Pivot

I moved the interaction surface from the 'Module' to the 'Document.' By anchoring workflows directly onto the drawing or model, the viewer became the operating system for the project, not just a window into a file.

  • Documents act as the anchor for all actions
  • Workflows and collaboration are embedded
  • Users move across 2D, 3D, and reality seamlessly
  • Designed for scale across large datasets and stakeholders

Strategic Trade-offs

Several structural and architectural decisions shaped the experience, balancing usability, scale, and system constraints.

Mode Logic: Separated 'Consumption' from 'Creation' to protect data integrity.

Contextual Persistence: Utilized side-drawers and overlays to ensure the document never left the user's field of vision.

Unified Viewer: Eliminated module switching that led to increased system complexity

In-context Collaboration: Allowed for improved visibility while requiring robust permissions

Solution

A multi-dimensional viewer combining navigation, review, and collaboration into a single workspace. Users move between observing, collaborating, and acting across various dimensions without losing context.

  • Layered viewing across document types
  • Integrated comparison and navigation
  • Context-aware tools
  • Embedded workflows and communication

Impact - Quantified Results

Simplified complex workflows and improved clarity across large-scale document ecosystems.

Spotify UX: A Case Study & Redesign (2024)

B2C Entertainment Independent Case Study

Purpose and Goals

This case study evaluates the user experience (UX) and interface (UI) design of Spotify Premium across web, Windows, and macOS platforms.
The objective is to identify critical friction points and structural inconsistencies that hinder user productivity and personalization, with the ultimate goal of proposing design solutions that better align the platform with actual user needs.

The Core User Requirements

The current iteration* of the Spotify desktop application prioritizes algorithmic discovery at the expense of user control and workflow efficiency. Based on my analysis, the core user requirements are:

  • Minimalist Focus: An environment free from irrelevant distractions or forced suggestions.
  • System Consistency: A predictable interface that maintains functional patterns across all screens.
  • Personalization: Granular control over library management and visibility, moving from a passive "what you might like" model to an active "what I actually like" experience.

*Spotify updated it's interface a few months after my case study was concluded. However, majority of the friction and pain points identified in it are still relevant.

Key UX Friction Points

Through a comprehensive audit of the interface, several systemic issues were identified:

  1. Navigation and Information Architecture
    • Inconsistent Hierarchy: The app lacks a clear page history, making navigation feel aimless. Back/forward buttons are often vague or non-functional.
    • Redundant/Confusing UI: Elements like the Homepage navigation and "Browse" buttons are placed in unconventional locations or exhibit overlapping, redundant behavior.
    • Overly Aggressive Search: The search function begins fetching results with the first keystroke, often returning wildly inaccurate suggestions. It lacks pre-filtering, forcing users to sift through irrelevant categories.
  2. Layout and Visual Consistency
    • Component Fragmentation: Features behave differently depending on where they are opened (e.g., in a center panel versus a side panel), creating a disjointed user journey.
    • Library Management: The "Your Library" panel is overly restrictive. Resizing options are limited, and the transition from list to grid view is poorly implemented, failing to provide users with meaningful control over how they organize their collection.
    • Visual Noise: Song details and thumbnails lack proper padding and distinct layout logic, leading to a cluttered, overwhelming visual state.
  3. Action Logic and Feedback
    • Ambiguous Icons: The use of checkmarks and plus icons is counter-intuitive. An icon may simultaneously represent "Add to Library", "Liked", and "Add to Playlist", leading to redundant steps and a lack of clarity regarding the current state of an item.
    • Opaque Data: The "Now Playing" screen fails to prioritize essential information like album titles or song credits, instead prioritizing the artist's general profile. The Artists' homepage also lack proper heirarchy, navigational aid, and coherence.

Conclusion: Form Over Function

The overarching issue is that the application currently prioritizes form over function. Despite being a premium subscription service, the platform offers surprisingly little in the way of deep user customization. The current design philosophy leans heavily into a reactive "what the user may like" model, which often clashes with the user's explicit intent to simply listen to and manage their personal library.
To improve the user experience, Spotify must strike a balance between Algorithmic Discovery and User Control. There's a need of focus toward providing a more consistent, coherent, and user-centric architecture that empowers the user to define their own experience.

Design Strategy & The Solution

My goal was to create a redesign that shifts the platform’s focus from a reactive "what you may like" model to an active "what I actually like" experience.
Here are the specific design solutions applied to create a more consistent, coherent, and user-centric architecture.

Foundation & The Design System

Some visual design nitty-gritty to start things off. The idea was to ensures coherence and efficient scaling.

  • Structured Scaling: Smallest division set to 8 pts; default padding/margins defined at 96 | 48 pts.
  • Consistent Iconography: Icons scaled consistently (48 | 24 pts general, 54 | 32 pts for play controls).
  • Font System: Standardization of Circular Spotify Text (Light, Medium, Bold, Black) across four distinct sizes (36, 24, 16, 14 pts).

Contextual Navigation & Layout

The overall application architecture was rationalized. The main hub is centered on the My Library panel, designed to feel powerful and personal. I created a modularized grid and list view for libraries, reducing restriction and granting meaningful scaling options. To combat navigation confusion, a persistent main hub now serves as the anchor, replacing vague, multi-panel behavior.

The "Focus-First" Player

A central goal was reducing visual clutter while maximizing user control. The persistent play controls and 'Now Playing' view were unified.

  • Clarity on Hover: Critical actions (About the artist, Share, Tour dates, etc.) are consolidated and accessed cleanly via an optimized context menu.
  • Dedicated Focus States: I created streamlined sub-views—like Connect to a device, Lyrics, and Add to Playlist—that load consistently in a side panel without shifting the entire layout. This reduces visual noise while keeping the user’s main focus intact.

Targeted Discovery & Reduced Search Noise

I streamlined discovery entry points, and added sub-navigations, context-based searches.

  • Discover Page: This dedicated view focuses on personalized and algorithmic recommendations ("Made For Shreyas"), freeing the library view of distraction.
  • Streamlined Search Results: The search function was optimized for accuracy. It provides immediate clarity by presenting results in a prioritized list and simplified interactions (play, contextual options). I removed immediate algorithmic pre-filters, placing initial filtering control (category buttons like 'All', 'Songs', 'Artists') directly in the user’s hands.

Final Conclusion: Function-First Spotify

This redesign establishes that personalization is not just about what a user passively streams. By prioritizing systematic consistency, functional clarity, and granular user control over algorithmic projection, this design creates a Spotify environment that is cohesive, respectful of user focus, and fundamentally function-first.
